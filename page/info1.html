Why Food Waste Matters

Food waste is a serious global issue with environmental, social, and economic consequences. According to resources from Urban Green Lab and the World Wildlife Fund, food waste contributes significantly to climate change due to methane emissions from landfills. At the same time, valuable resources like water, energy, and labor used to produce, package, and transport that food are wasted. Educational initiatives emphasize that much of this waste is preventable. Simple strategies such as proper meal planning, understanding expiration dates, and repurposing leftovers can help households reduce their waste. Better Homes & Gardens also encourages using scraps like vegetable stems, fruit peels, and bread ends in creative recipes rather than tossing them. Through small, mindful habits at home, schools, and communities, we can reduce food waste and make a meaningful impact on sustainability.
